About Park

I am a doctoral candidate in Operations Research at the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Prof. Gad Allon. My primary research interests are in the design and analysis of data-driven and human-centric solutions to problems in operations management, currently focusing on sharing economy, flexible workforce, team dynamics, dynamic pricing, and urban analytics. I earned my ScB in Computer Engineering and Applied Mathematics-Economics from Brown University and my SM in Computation for Design and Optimization from MIT, where I worked with Prof. Georgia Perakis and Prof. Y. Karen Zheng.

Prior to Wharton, I worked as a software engineer at Oracle, a strats analyst at Goldman Sachs, and a data scientist at Deloitte Consulting. At Penn, I am actively involved with various organizations, from participating in the Intercultural Leadership Program to developing marketing strategies for TEDxPenn to advising undergraduate students at Stouffer College House. Growing up in Bangkok, I am a world traveller, avid foodie, and aspiring creative artist.
